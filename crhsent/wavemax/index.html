A word on review-gating — a real liability

"Review funnel" widgets that route happy customers to public review sites while diverting unhappy ones to a private form are review-gating. That practice violates Google's terms and is reachable under the FTC's Trade Regulation Rule on Consumer Reviews — civil penalties run to roughly $53,000 per violation. If a review control is added to your site without proper diligence, the liability lands on the operator. Mine are built to funnel compliantly, never to gate. Ask me how it should be done.

This already happened. WaveMAX's current marketing partner published a review-gating control on every franchise page and left it live for weeks. It took a franchisee flagging the liability to get it pulled from the Austin page — and multiple more warnings before it came off the rest. Every operator wore that exposure the entire time.
