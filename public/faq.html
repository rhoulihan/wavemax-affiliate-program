Money

Investment range, qualification, financing, and what franchisees actually earn.

Per the 2025 FDD, total investment ranges from $356,455 on the low end (smaller market, smaller buildout) to $1,556,350 on the high end (larger market, premium-tier equipment). Typical builds in mid-tier markets land $1M–$1.5M. We look for $400K of liquidity to qualify; the balance is typically SBA-financed. See the Why Invest page for the full Item 19 detail.
Per the 2025 FDD Item 19 disclosure: a 50-store sample of WaveMAX locations reporting full-year 2024 grossed an average of $471,201, median $436,114, top performer $713,934. Reported EBITDA across the system runs 25–35%. Our company-owned Jacksonville flagship reported 2024 net cash flow of $217,762 and net income of $113,074 (with full equipment financing). Past performance is not a guarantee of future results — see the FDD for full disclosures.
WaveMAX is SBA-eligible, and we maintain a network of preferred lenders familiar with the brand and its Item 19 results. Most franchisees finance equipment fully and put down 20–30% on the buildout. We make introductions and walk you through the lender process during discovery (step 03 of the buying process).
5–7 years is the typical payback period across the franchise system, driven by 25–35% reported EBITDA and the multi-channel revenue mix (self-serve + WDF + commercial routes + delivery). Higher-volume stores in stronger markets have reported faster paybacks; the FDD Item 19 has the full distribution.

Process

Discovery, FDD review, site selection, training, opening.

Six steps: (1) initial 30-minute exploratory call, (2) FDD review and signed receipt, (3) market analysis and territory selection, (4) Discovery Day at corporate HQ in Jacksonville, (5) franchise agreement, (6) training, site buildout, and grand opening. See the Become a Franchisee page for the step-by-step detail.
Typical timeline is 6–9 months from initial call to grand opening. The pace is mostly site-availability- and buildout-driven; if a candidate already has a site identified and pre-approved, the timeline compresses. The training program itself is multi-week.

Operations

Owner involvement, staffing, customers, daily reality.

Most franchisees don't hire a dedicated manager initially; the model is structured so multi-unit owners scale without one.

Equipment

Fleet specs, premium tiers, what's included.

Modern Electrolux soft-mount fleet with programmable controllers, online diagnostics, and high-G extract for faster cycles (full load done in <45 minutes). Premium-tier stores include 450G extract, 80lb large-format machines for comforters / restaurant linens / commercial pickups, and UV-sanitization-equipped lines for medical and hospitality customers. Equipment package and store-specific premium features vary by location and approved tier.
Soft-mount construction (quieter, less wear on building), high-G extract (faster cycle, lower utility cost per pound), programmable controllers (every cycle the same), online diagnostics (remote troubleshooting, predictive maintenance), and a corporate-negotiated parts and service relationship. The total cost of ownership over 10 years is meaningfully lower than legacy hard-mount equipment.

Growth & Exit

Multi-unit pathway, territory rights, what a sale looks like.

Multi-unit ownership is the path most franchisees take. Operators with one cash-flowing store typically open their second within 18 months. Territory rights and area-development incentives are available for qualified multi-unit operators. The site selection, training, and operations playbooks are designed to be repeated — we explicitly built the system to support multi-unit growth, not fight it.
A WaveMAX sells as a franchised cash-flow business — typically a multiple of EBITDA, with brand goodwill, established recurring WDF and commercial contracts, training transferable to the buyer, and corporate support continuing post-sale. That contrasts sharply with the legacy independent laundromat, which often sells for the depreciated value of its equipment package alone.
