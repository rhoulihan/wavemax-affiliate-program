Why this category

Six structural reasons operators choose laundry.

Beyond the numbers, the laundromat category has structural characteristics that make it a durable cash-flow business — particularly when modernized, professionalized, and run as a franchise system rather than a one-off independent operation.

Recession-resistant demand

Laundry is a need, not a want. Working-class families rely on laundromats out of necessity — usage patterns are stable through every economic cycle.

No inventory risk

The "product" is a service, not goods. No spoilage, no obsolescence, no inventory writedowns. Equipment depreciates predictably and is well-understood by lenders.

Cash-pay business

No accounts receivable. No collections. No 30-day payment terms. The model converts revenue to cash on the same day it's earned.

Lean staffing

Modern attended-but-not-coin-operated stores run with a small team. Labor cost is a smaller percentage of revenue than retail or food service.

SBA-eligible financing

Equipment + build-out are SBA-eligible. Lenders who understand laundry economics structure financing accordingly. Most franchisees use a mix of cash + SBA debt.

Multi-unit pathway

The model is built for scale. Operators with one cash-flowing store typically open their second within 18 months, supported by area-development incentives.

External validation
