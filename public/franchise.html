Frequently asked

WaveMAX® franchise FAQ

Is WaveMAX really passive income?

It's a semi-absentee business from day one. We recommend being in the store about 9 hours per week — 3 days a week, 3 hours per day. Most franchisees don't hire a dedicated manager initially; the model is designed to scale without one.

Who are your core customers?

Blue-collar, working-class families who rely on laundromats out of necessity, not luxury. The customer is steady through every economic cycle. We also serve Airbnb hosts, restaurants, gyms, and medical offices on the commercial side.

What's the total investment?

Total investment runs $1M–$1.5M; we look for $400K of liquidity to qualify. That covers franchise fee, build-out, equipment, working capital, and opening marketing. Item 19 of the FDD has the detailed breakdown.

Will WaveMAX help me find a site?

Yes. We guide you through working-class ZIP codes, demographic targeting, and brand-approved locations before lease sign-off. Site selection is the single highest-leverage decision in this business and we don't leave it to chance.

How big is WaveMAX right now?

65 stores and growing, supported by quality unit economics, franchisee satisfaction, and a scalable national playbook. We build durable, cash-flowing businesses — not just franchises.

Can I open multiple locations?

Multi-unit ownership is the path most franchisees take. Operators with one cash-flowing store typically open their second within 18 months. Territory rights and area-development incentives are available for serious multi-unit operators.
