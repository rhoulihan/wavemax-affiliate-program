Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 20, 2026  ·  Last Updated: May 20, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how CRHS Enterprises, LLC ("we," "us," "our"), the independently-owned operator of WaveMAX Laundry Austin, collects, uses, shares, and protects your personal information when you use our websites and services.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to all sites we operate, including:

  • rundberglaundry.com (primary)
  • atxwashdryfold.com
  • atxwashateria.com
  • runberglaundry.com
  • wavemax.promo (legacy redirects to the primary site)

WaveMAX™ is a trademark of WaveMAX Franchise, LLC. Our location is independently owned and operated by CRHS Enterprises, LLC under a franchise license from WaveMAX Franchise, LLC. This Privacy Policy is between you and CRHS Enterprises, LLC.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

When you register as a customer, affiliate, or operator we collect:

  • Name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • Account credentials (username, password — passwords are stored as PBKDF2 hashes, never plaintext)
  • Payment-related identifiers (PayPal email, Venmo handle, CashApp tag, or business banking details — all stored encrypted with AES-256-GCM)
  • Business / W-9 information for affiliate payouts (tax ID, business name, signed W-9 document)
  • Service preferences (pickup instructions, language)
  • Content you submit through contact forms or messages

2.2 OAuth / Social Login Data (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn)

If you choose to sign in with Google, Facebook, or LinkedIn, the provider sends us a limited set of profile fields based on the scopes you authorize. We request only the scopes necessary to identify your account and contact you:

  • Google: scopes openid, email, profile — we receive your Google account ID, primary email, name, and (if public) profile picture URL.
  • Facebook: permissions email, public_profile — we receive your Facebook user ID, email, name, and profile picture URL.
  • LinkedIn: scopes r_emailaddress, r_liteprofile — we receive your LinkedIn member ID, email, and name.

We do not request access to your friends list, contacts, posts, calendar, or any other data beyond the fields listed above. We do not post on your behalf. We do not sell or share OAuth-derived profile data with advertisers or data brokers.

OAuth access and refresh tokens are stored encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM, are scoped to the originating provider, and are used only to keep your account linked. We do not call provider APIs on a recurring basis except to refresh the access token.

2.3 Information Collected Automatically

  • IP address, browser type and version, operating system
  • Pages visited, referrer URL, time stamps
  • Cookies and similar identifiers (see §10 — Cookies)
  • Approximate geolocation derived from IP (city-level)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Create and maintain your account; authenticate you on return visits
  • Process orders, schedule pickups, deliver wash-dry-fold service
  • Calculate and pay affiliate commissions; comply with IRS tax-reporting requirements (1099-NEC)
  • Send transactional emails (order confirmation, pickup reminders, payment receipts)
  • Respond to customer support inquiries
  • Detect, prevent, and investigate fraud, abuse, or security incidents
  • Comply with legal obligations (subpoenas, court orders, tax law)
  • Improve our services through aggregate analytics
  • Send promotional emails — only with your opt-in consent and only until you unsubscribe

We do not use your information for automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you, and we do not engage in profiling for advertising purposes.

4. How We Share Your Information

We share personal information only with:

  • Service providers who help us run the business — email delivery (Mailcow, Brevo), document signing (DocuSign for W-9), payment processors (Venmo, PayPal, CashApp when you complete a payment to us via those platforms), maps and reviews (Google Places API), and hosting infrastructure. Each is contractually bound to handle your data only as we direct.
  • Affiliates — your assigned WaveMAX Austin affiliate receives the contact and order details needed to schedule and complete your service.
  • Law enforcement or government agencies — only when legally required and only the minimum data responsive to a valid order.
  • Successors — if our business is acquired or merged, your information may transfer to the successor, who will be bound by this Privacy Policy or a successor policy with at least equivalent protections.

We do not sell personal information to advertisers, data brokers, or any third party. We do not "share" personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined by California, Colorado, Connecticut, or Virginia privacy laws.

5. Data Retention

We keep your information only as long as needed:

  • Account profile and order history: while your account is active, plus 3 years after closure for tax and dispute records.
  • W-9 forms and 1099-NEC backup: 7 years (IRS retention requirement).
  • Payment-method identifiers (PayPal email, Venmo handle): until you remove them from your account, or 3 years after account closure.
  • OAuth tokens: rotated regularly and deleted on logout or account deletion.
  • Server logs (IP, user-agent): 90 days, then aggregated or deleted.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Regardless of where you live, you may:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information through your account profile, or by contacting us.
  • Delete your account and all associated personal data (see §7 below for the data-deletion process).
  • Withdraw consent for promotional emails by clicking "unsubscribe" in any email, or by contacting us.
  • Disconnect a social login at any time from your account settings; the provider's profile data is removed from our records when you disconnect.
  • Object to specific uses of your information.
  • Export a machine-readable copy of your data (data portability).

California residents (CCPA/CPRA): you additionally have the right to know the categories of personal information we collect, the categories of sources, the business or commercial purpose, and the categories of third parties we share with; the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights; and the right to designate an authorized agent.

EU/UK residents (GDPR): our legal basis for processing is generally (i) performance of a contract (for service delivery), (ii) consent (for marketing), or (iii) legitimate interests (for security, fraud prevention, and analytics). You may lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority.

7. How to Delete Your Data

You can permanently delete your account and all associated personal data in two ways:

  1. From your account: sign in, open your account settings, choose "Delete all my data." You'll be prompted to confirm; deletion is irreversible and completes within 24 hours.
  2. By email: send a request from your registered email address to privacy@rundberglaundry.com with the subject line "Data Deletion Request." We respond within 30 days and confirm in writing when deletion is complete.

Facebook-specific data-deletion callback: if you signed in with Facebook and want to remove only the Facebook-derived data we hold (while keeping your other account data), visit your account settings → Connected accounts → Disconnect Facebook, or send the request above and specify "Facebook OAuth data only."

Some records (signed W-9s, transaction history) are retained for the legally-required period set out in §5 even after account deletion; we keep only what we are legally required to keep, and never use it for any other purpose.

8. Security

We protect your information using:

  • TLS 1.2+ encryption in transit on every page and API call.
  • AES-256-GCM encryption at rest for OAuth tokens, payment-method identifiers, and W-9 documents.
  • PBKDF2-SHA512 (100,000 iterations) for password hashing.
  • Role-based access control; only personnel with a business need can access account data.
  • Rate limiting, CSRF tokens, mongo-injection sanitization, and strict Content Security Policy.
  • Encrypted backups; physical data center security at our cloud provider.

No system is perfectly secure. If we ever experience a breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us at privacy@rundberglaundry.com and we will delete it.

10. Cookies and Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies for:

  • Strictly necessary: session, CSRF protection, login state. These cannot be disabled.
  • Functional: language preference, last-viewed page. Improves your return experience.
  • Analytics: aggregate page-view counts. We do not use cross-site tracking.

We do not use advertising cookies, behavioral re-targeting pixels, or cross-site identifiers. We honor your browser's Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal as a request to opt out of any data "sale" or "sharing" under state law.

11. International Data Transfers

We operate primarily in the United States. If you access our services from outside the U.S., your information will be transferred to and processed in the U.S., where data-protection laws may differ from those in your country. By using our services you consent to this transfer.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last Updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated by email or prominent notice on this site before they take effect. Continued use of the service after the effective date constitutes acceptance.
